Ingredients:
CONDENSED MILK: 1/2 piece
LIMES: 5 tbsp
EGGS: 2 piece
BUTTER (MARGARINE): 43 g
SUGAR: 18 g
COOKIES (GOYA MARIA): 1/2 bag

Preparation:
Step 1
Place rack in the center of oven and preheat to 350Â°F.
Step 2
Pulse goya cookies, sugar, and pinch of salt in a food processor until fine crumbs form. With the motor running, drizzle in melted butter and process until well-combined. Transfer mixture to pie pan and press into bottom and up sides with a flat-bottomed measuring cup.
Step 3
Bake pie crust until golden brown and set, 8 minutes. Transfer to a wire rack and let cool to room temperature, about 15 minutes.
Step 4
Whisk condensed milk and yolks together in a large bowl. Add Key lime and lemon juices and whisk until well-combined (mixture will thicken slightly).
Step 5
Pour filling into crust and bake until center is just set, about 15 minutes. Transfer to the refrigerator for at least 30 min.
Step 6
Use the white eggs to make the meringue: weight the white eggs, then add double the amount of sugar, then use boiling water to heat up the eggs, une one of the wisks from the manual whisk at minimun level of speed for aprox 8 minutes until the mix look white and you cannot feel the sugar, then take it out of the heat and mix it at full level of speed with both whisk for aprox another 8 to 10 min.
Step 7
Pipe on top of the pie and then bake the pie for another 10-15 min at 350.
Step 8
Transfer pie to a wire rack and let cool completely (filling will set as it cools), about 1 hour. Transfer to refrigerator and chill until cold, at least 1 hour.

The recipe is for two small pie pans.

Each pan need 76gr of the cookie mix from step 1.
